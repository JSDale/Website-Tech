My Routine

Washing:
My hair routine is now very simplistic. Once a week I do a scalp treatment and nutrition mask overnight. I then wash these out the next day with a natural shampoo and conditioner. I always use cool water when washing my hair to prevent further damage and frizz. When applying the shampoo, I use a scalp massager to both exfoliate my scalp as well as help to promote hair growth. I will apply shampoo twice to only my scalp, before applying conditioner. I will only apply conditioner to the ends of my hair and leave it on for a while. At the end of my shower, I will rinse my hair with cold water and occasionally I will do an apple cider vinegar hair rinse.

When I step out of the shower, I’ll squeeze out any excess water and loosely wrap my hair in a microfibre towel turban. Once I am dressed, I will remove the towel and apply the hair primer and leave-in conditioner to my hair. I comb out any tangles and leave to air dry.

Once my hair is dry, I’ll apply a couple drops of hair oil to the ends of my hair. This helps to reduce frizz and adds shine.

In between my weekly wash, I’ll cleanse my scalp if I need to with a co-wash. I find this really helps to freshen up my hair without drying the ends out, compared to if I were to wash with shampoo. I will do this every 2-3 days and my after shower hair care remains the same.

Daily Haircare:
To prevent damage to my hair, I will keep it in a protective hairstyle most of the time. These include braids and buns. I’ll either use an invisible scrunchy or hair stick. These don’t pull on hair and they can very stylish. I will regularly apply a hair oil to the ends of my hair if I need to.

When I go to bed, I brush my hair. Starting from the bottom, gently with my wooden brush. I apply a little hair oil, then braid my hair loosely securing with a soft scrunchy. Unless I am doing an overnight hair mask, I will sleep on a silk pillowcase.

Monthly Haircare:
Every month I will do the ‘search and destroy’ method. To do this, I divide my hair into inch wide sections and search for any split ends along the length of my hair. When I find one, I snip the split end using a sharp pair of scissors (use scissors responsibly). This can be a long process, but also a therapeutic one.
